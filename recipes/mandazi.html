Simple Mandazi Recipe

Description

Mandazi(Swahili: Mandazi, Maandazi), is a form of fried bread that originated on the Swahili Coast.

It is also known as bofrot or puff puff in Western African countries such as Ghana and Nigeria

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour (375 g), plus more for dusting
  • ½ cup sugar (100 g)
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 2 teaspoons ground cardamom
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 can oil, for frying

    • Some Optional Requirements Include:

  • 13 ½ oz coconut milk (400 mL)

Preparation

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, cardamom, and salt.
  2. Make a well in the center, then use a rubber spatula to mix in the egg and coconut milk until the dough comes together.
  3. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead until smooth.
  4. Dive the dough into 4 portions. Roll out 1 portion of dough into a circle about ¼-inch (6 ml) thick.
  5. Cut into 8 triangles. Repeat with the remaining dough portions.
  6. Heat the oil in a large pot until it reaches 350˚F (180˚C).
  7. Working in batches, fry the mandazi for 2-3 minutes per side, until golden brown.
  8. Drain on paper towels.
  9. Serve as breakfast or a snack with 5. Kenyan coffee or chai.
